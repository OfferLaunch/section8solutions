What Is Section 8 Housing?

The Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher Program is a federal initiative that provides housing assistance to low-income families, seniors, and people with disabilities.

Here's how it works in practice:

  • Tenants apply for and wait (sometimes years) to receive a Section 8 voucher.
  • Once approved, the government covers a large portion of their rent â€” often 70â€“100%.
  • Rent is paid directly to landlords each month, typically on the 1st, via the local housing authority.

For property owners, this means:

  • Guaranteed payments (the government is your tenant).
  • High tenant demand (waitlists for vouchers can be years long).
  • Stability, since tenants don't want to lose their voucher.
